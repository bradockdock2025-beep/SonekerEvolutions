## THE GOLDEN RULE BEFORE ANYTHING

**The posts that get engagement are NOT about your features.**
They are about **your journey, your honesty, your thinking, and your results.**

Features → nobody cares.
Struggles + wins + insights → people follow, share, and buy.

The formula for every post:
> **Specific situation** + **honest reaction** + **what you did / learned**

## CATEGORY 2 — PRODUCT DECISION POSTS
*These show your thinking. Engineers and founders LOVE these. High engagement.*

---

**2.1 — Why you chose Claude AI**
```
Soneker is powered by Claude AI, not GPT.

Here's why:

1. Long structured outputs — Claude is significantly better at maintaining complex JSON structure across long responses
2. Knowledge extraction precision — Claude understands "what's the conceptual framework here?" better
3. Instruction following — when I say "extract exactly 3 tags per card," Claude does it. GPT drifts.

I tested both for 3 weeks.
Claude won for this specific use case.
```

---

**2.2 — Why you built a Knowledge Map**
```
The Knowledge Map in Soneker was never in the original plan.

I added it because of one question that kept nagging me:

"Okay, I have 15 knowledge cards. How do they RELATE to each other?"

A list of concepts is useful.
A visual graph of how they connect is a superpower.

Nobody asked for it.
I built it anyway.

It's now the feature users mention the most.
```

---

**2.3 — A feature you almost cut**
```
I almost cut Smart Vocabulary from Soneker.

Felt like a nice-to-have. Too much extra work for launch.

Then I showed a demo to someone learning English.

They stopped me mid-sentence.

"Wait — it automatically captures every word I didn't know, with context?"

That's when I understood: Smart Vocabulary isn't a feature for learners.
It IS the product for language learners.

Kept it. Expanding it.
```

---

**2.4 — A technical decision and why**
```
Soneker generates knowledge in real-time via Claude API.

The first version waited until everything was ready, then showed it all at once.

Users hated it. The wait felt like nothing was happening.

So I added a loading screen with 4 steps:
"Fetching transcript → Analyzing content → Building knowledge → Mapping concepts"

Same wait time. Completely different feeling.

UX is perception engineering.
```

---

**2.5 — Why 4 languages from day one**
```
Most SaaS tools launch in English only.

Soneker launched in 4: PT, EN, FR, DE.

Why?

Because I'm Portuguese.
And I'm tired of tools that treat non-English speakers as an afterthought.

Every string in the product is fully localized.
Not Google Translated. Actually written.

If your audience speaks multiple languages: respect that from day one.
```

---

**2.6 — A decision you regret**
```
Biggest mistake in the first version of Soneker:

I set the AI output token limit too low to save costs.

Result: the Knowledge Map would come back empty. No data.

The first 2 user analyses looked like the product was broken.

It wasn't broken. I was just being cheap with tokens.

Lesson: don't optimize for cost before you optimize for experience.
```

---

**2.7 — Why PDF export matters**
```
Why did I spend 3 days getting the PDF export right?

Because a PDF is what gets shared.

When someone sends "here are my notes from that video," they send a PDF.

Every PDF has "Generated by Soneker" in the footer.

Every PDF is marketing.

The export isn't just a feature. It's a distribution channel.
```

## CATEGORY 5 — CONTROVERSIAL / OPINION POSTS
*These start conversations. High engagement. Post 2x per week.*

---

**5.1 — Against AI summarizers**
```
Hot take: AI video summarizers are mostly useless.

A summary tells you what was said.
It doesn't tell you:

→ The underlying structure of the argument
→ Which concepts are actually connected
→ What vocabulary you need to understand the domain
→ What you should DO with this information

Summaries compress content.
Knowledge architecture extracts structure.

These are completely different products.
```

---

**5.2 — On building features nobody asked for**
```
Controversial product opinion:

Don't only build what users ask for.

Users tell you their symptoms.
Not their diagnosis.

"Make it faster" might mean "the loading screen makes me anxious."
"Add more features" might mean "I don't understand the value I'm already getting."
"Can you integrate with X?" might mean "my current workflow is broken."

Listen to what they ask.
Build what they need.
```

---

**5.3 — On launching before you're ready**
```
The best decision I made building Soneker:

Launching before I was ready.

The version I launched with:
→ No onboarding
→ No mobile optimization
→ One export format
→ Zero marketing

The version I almost waited for:
→ Perfect onboarding flow
→ 5 export formats
→ Full analytics dashboard

If I had waited for the "complete" version, I'd still be building it.

The first version taught me more in 2 weeks than 6 months of planning.
```

---

**5.4 — On retention vs. acquisition**
```
Most apps spend 90% of effort on acquisition.

The ones that survive spend 60% on retention.

Getting a user is hard.
Keeping a user is harder.
A user who stays is your marketing.

Soneker's retention strategy:
Every time they use it, they get something genuinely useful.
Not notifications. Not gamification.
Just: open it, paste a link, get something valuable.

Retention = delivering the core promise, every single time.
```

---

**5.5 — On pricing**
```
Unpopular opinion on SaaS pricing:

Your free plan should be good enough that people feel guilty not paying.

Not crippled.
Not a 7-day trial.
Genuinely good.

Why? Because people who get value from the free plan become your best advocates.
They tweet about it. They recommend it. They convert when their needs grow.

A bad free plan builds resentment.
A good free plan builds trust.
```

## CATEGORY 9 — EDUCATIONAL POSTS ABOUT YOUR DOMAIN
*Teach about learning, retention, and knowledge management. Positions you as an expert.*

---

**9.1 — The forgetting curve**
```
Ebbinghaus discovered this in 1885:

Without review, you forget:
→ 50% of new information within 1 hour
→ 70% within 24 hours
→ 90% within a week

This is called the Forgetting Curve.

It applies to everything you watch on YouTube.

The only way to beat it:
→ Active processing (not passive watching)
→ Structured output (not loose notes)
→ Review (going back to what you captured)

Soneker handles the first two automatically.
```

---

**9.2 — On note-taking methods**
```
The 4 most popular note-taking methods, ranked by how well they work for VIDEO:

4. Cornell Method — great for lectures, slow for video
3. Mind mapping — great for structure, terrible if done during video
2. The Zettelkasten — excellent for connection, requires discipline
1. AI extraction → verify → connect — fastest path from video to retained knowledge

The future of learning from video isn't better note-taking.
It's better extraction + your verification layer.
```

---

**9.3 — On second brains**
```
The "second brain" concept is everywhere right now.

But most second brains have the same problem:

The INPUT is broken.

→ Books: Readwise handles this
→ Articles: Instapaper, Reader
→ Podcasts: getting better
→ **YouTube: almost nothing good exists**

YouTube is the most information-dense medium in existence.
And it's the most underserved in the second brain ecosystem.

That gap is why Soneker exists.
```
